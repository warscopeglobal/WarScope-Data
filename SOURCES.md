# Approved Data Sources

All data contributed to warscope-data must be sourced from verifiable, publicly available sources.

---

## Free Sources (Preferred)

### SIPRI
- **URL:** https://sipri.org
- **Best for:** Arms transfers (TIV database), military expenditure
- **Citation:** `SIPRI Arms Transfers Database, accessed March 2026`

### UCDP — Uppsala Conflict Data Program
- **URL:** https://ucdp.uu.se
- **Best for:** Conflict history, battle deaths, start/end dates
- **Citation:** `UCDP Conflict Catalog v24.1`
- **Note:** Academic gold standard — preferred over all other conflict sources

### CIA World Factbook
- **URL:** https://cia.gov/the-world-factbook/
- **Best for:** Military branches, personnel overview, defence budget
- **Citation:** `CIA World Factbook 2026 — [Country Name]`

### FAS Nuclear Notebook
- **URL:** https://fas.org/issues/nuclear-weapons/nuclear-notebook/
- **Best for:** Nuclear warhead counts, delivery systems
- **Citation:** `FAS Nuclear Notebook — [Country], [Month Year]`

### ACLED
- **URL:** https://acleddata.com
- **Best for:** Recent conflict events, casualties, locations
- **Citation:** `ACLED Data — [Country], accessed March 2026`

### OFAC SDN List (US Sanctions)
- **URL:** https://ofac.treasury.gov
- **Best for:** US sanctions — financial, arms, travel
- **Citation:** `OFAC SDN List + Executive Order [number], [date]`

### EU Sanctions Map
- **URL:** https://sanctionsmap.eu
- **Best for:** EU sanctions regimes
- **Citation:** `EU Sanctions Map — Council Regulation [number/year]`

### UN Security Council Sanctions
- **URL:** https://un.org/securitycouncil/sanctions/information
- **Best for:** UN-mandated arms embargoes
- **Citation:** `UN Security Council Resolution [number] ([year])`

### GlobalFirepower
- **URL:** https://globalfirepower.com
- **Important:** Cross-reference ONLY — always verify against a primary source. Never use as sole source.
- **Citation:** `GlobalFirepower 2025 (cross-reference — verified against [primary source])`

### Government Defence Ministry Websites
- **Best for:** Official personnel figures, unit names, equipment lists
- **Examples:** defense.gov (USA), mod.gov.uk (UK), army.mil.ng (Nigeria)
- **Citation:** `[Service] official website — [URL], accessed [date]`

### Manufacturer Datasheets
- **Best for:** Vehicle and weapon specifications
- **Citation:** `[Manufacturer] official specifications — [URL]`

### Major News Outlets
- **Approved:** Reuters, AP, BBC, Al Jazeera, Defense News, Breaking Defense
- **Citation:** `Reuters, "[Title]", [Date], [URL]`

---

## Paywalled Sources (Cite but Never Copy Text)

### IISS Military Balance
- Cite page numbers and figures only. Do NOT copy paragraphs — copyright violation.
- **Citation:** `IISS Military Balance 2025, p.[page]`

### Jane's Defence
- Same rules as IISS.
- **Citation:** `Jane's World Armies — [Country], [Year]`

---

## Not Approved

| Source | Reason |
|--------|---------|
| Social media (Twitter, Facebook, Telegram) | Unverifiable |
| Reddit, military forums | Unverifiable, often speculation |
| Leaked or classified documents | Legal risk |
| Anonymous blogs | No accountability |
| AI-generated content | Cannot verify |
| Personal estimates presented as facts | Misleading |
